**Sign-off authority**: 
- Manufacturing lead (certifies all components manufactured per spec)
- QC manager (certifies all testing completed and passed)

## SECTION 5: POST-HANDOFF RESPONSIBILITIES

### 5.1 Manufacturer Responsibilities (Post-Handoff)

**Warranty period**: 6 months from acceptance date

During warranty period:
- Respond to mechanical failure reports within 48 hours
- Provide remote troubleshooting support
- Replace defective components if failure traced to manufacturing defect
- Provide spare parts list and sourcing information
- Document any field failures for design improvement

**Post-warranty support**:
- Provide historical and technical consultation
- Advise on museum curation and preservation
- Support research projects and educational initiatives
- Provide expert guidance on repairs or restoration

### 5.2 Recipient Responsibilities (Post-Handoff)

**Operational maintenance** (per Phase 3 Operational Manual):
- Daily pre-operation inspection (15 minutes)
- Weekly maintenance (45-60 minutes)
- Monthly lubrication and inspection (2-3 hours)
- Quarterly disassembly inspection (4-6 hours)
- Annual complete overhaul (30-40 hours)

**Record keeping**:
- Maintain operation log (date, programs executed, results, any issues)
- Document maintenance activities
- Report any unusual sounds, resistance, or mechanical issues
- Maintain spare parts inventory

**Protection and preservation**:
- Keep engine in climate-controlled environment (20-25°C, < 50% humidity)
- Protect from dust and debris
- Use desiccant in storage if environment is humid
- Periodically rotate bearing and lever mechanisms to prevent stiction

## CONCLUSION

The Babbage Analytical Engine project is now complete. All manufacturing, assembly, and testing phases have been successfully executed. The engine has been validated as fully operational and is ready for educational, research, or museum purposes. With comprehensive documentation and proven mechanical reliability, the project demonstrates the feasibility of manufacturing a Babbage Analytical Engine using industrial capabilities available to developing nations in the 1930s-1960s era.

This achievement validates that computation is hardware-independent, technology transfer is achievable, and complex historical engineering projects can be successfully reconstructed with modern specification and validation disciplines.
