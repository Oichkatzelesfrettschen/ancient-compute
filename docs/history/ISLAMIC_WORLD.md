Islamic World: Transmission Networks (Primary/Secondary Sources)
- Encyclopaedia of Islam (Brill) entries on Bayt al-Hikma and translators
- Bibliotheca Arabica manuscript catalogs
- JSTOR articles on Abbasid translation movement
- Bayt al-Hikma (House of Wisdom), Baghdad: records of translation under al-Ma'mun; cataloged in Gutas, Dimitri (1998) Greek Thought, Arabic Culture.
- Toledo School of Translators (12th c.): Gerard of Cremona translations (Ptolemy Almagest, Al-Khwarizmi); source: Burnett, Charles (2001) "The Coherence of the Arabic-Latin Translation Program in Toledo".
- Al-Biruni (c.1030): India (Kitab al-Hind) documenting Indian numerals and methods.
- Al-Khwarizmi (c.830): Kitab al-jabr wa-l-muqabala; Latin tradition (Algoritmi).
